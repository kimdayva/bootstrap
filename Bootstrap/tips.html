How to get started...

1. Everyone wants to get organized, but very few people know where to begin. Not everyone was born to be organized, but it's a skill that anyone can learn with a bit of practice and hard work. The key is making it a habit.

2. What steps should you take to get started? Well, this first step is acknoledging that there's a better way to do things. You've identified that your current system (if you have a "system") isn't working. Maybe you're forgetting appointments or misplacing important papers on a regular basis. Finding a central location for your thoughts, ideas, and agenda in a planner will help you take control of your time, and creating a home for all of your belongs (and putting them there when they're not being used) will help you take back your space.

3. You're here, so you must be ready for help controlling the chaos. The biggest hurdle is realizing that you don't need your entire, complete new system right from the beginning. It's a process. There will be some trial and error along the way.

Answer these few questions:

  1. What parts of my life do I need to organize? Be specific.
  2. Do I need to write things down on paper or have them available digitally?
  3. How detailed do I need my agenda to be? Consider other family members, work responsibilities, extracurricular activities, etc.
